    Self-introduction

    University of Chicago · Applied Data Science

    Self-introduction

    Hi, I'm Ryan.   6. 06 · Group B

    Yu-Hsuan Tung 董育瑄

    National Taiwan University · Mechanical Engineering (graduated)

    Self-introduction

    Hi, I'm Joyce. My research applies quantum computation to drug design, particularly for Alzheimer's disease, using quantum-chemistry methods to model the interaction energy between drug candidates and target pathogenic proteins, with the goal of mapping these problems onto quantum computers. I'm also interested in broader medical applications of quantum computation, including brain imaging and connectomics, as well as quantum machine learning and quantum algorithms.

  7. 07 · Group C

    Tiffany Jayi Pan 潘家怡

    University of Toronto · Pharmacology & Toxicology (MSc)

    Self-introduction

    I'm a recent MSc graduate from the University of Toronto's Department of Pharmacology and Toxicology. My research focused on the neuropsychiatric symptoms of Alzheimer's disease, including the therapeutic potential of cannabinoids and the role of neuroinflammation, particularly astrogliosis, in the pathophysiology of agitation in Alzheimer's disease. I hope to expand my knowledge through this program and apply it to drug development and clinical trials in Taiwan.

  8. 08 · Group C

    Cho-Ming Lee 李卓明

    National Taiwan University · College of Medicine, Physiology

    Self-introduction

    At NTU's Institute of Physiology I study ion channels, neuroscience and in-vivo electrophysiology, using them to dissect how anti-epileptic drugs work. Through this program I hope to learn how AI and quantum techniques apply to neuroscience and drug development, and to fold what I learn back into my research.
