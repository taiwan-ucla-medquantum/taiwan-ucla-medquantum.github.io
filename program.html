01 The Brief · 計畫綱要

A field study at the edge of medicine and the quantum.

Nine delegates from Taiwan spend sixty days at UCLA — from July 6 to September 3, 2026 — reading the working edge where biomedical engineering meets quantum science. This site is their field journal.

The program is part of Taiwan's Youth Overseas Dream Fund (青年百億海外圓夢基金計畫), the Taiwan Global Pathfinders Initiative run by the Ministry of Education's Youth Development Administration. It is organized by Asia University (亞洲大學) with partner TAITA-East (美東台美產業科技協會), hosted academically by the UCLA Center for Quantum Science & Engineering (CQSE), co-organized with UCLA Samueli Engineering, the David Geffen School of Medicine, and the UCLA Joe C. Wen School of Nursing.

It is led by Professor Kang L. Wang (王康隆), Distinguished Professor at UCLA and a director of CQSE. The delegation is small by design — nine students, mixed in age and discipline — so that every person presents, questions, and builds rather than spectates.

02 Four Signals · 四大主題

Four fields, read honestly — and the legend for what follows.

Each theme maps to a waveform that recurs in the rail as you read. Two are coloured for medicine, two for the quantum.

Field A · Medicine

AI × Brain & Neuroscience

The most clinically mature thread. Deep learning reads MRI/PET and, in 2025, the first blood test for Alzheimer's was cleared in the US — yet the field treats AI as decision-support, not autonomous diagnosis. Honest framing over hype.

EEG sleep-spindle

Field B · Medicine

AI × Drug Discovery & 中醫

AI is accelerating pre-clinical work and is now reaching into Traditional Chinese Medicine (中醫) through network pharmacology. As of 2025 no AI-discovered drug has reached market — the promise is real, the timeline sober.

mass-spectrum peaks

Field C · Quantum

Quantum Computing × Molecular Prediction

The least mature, the most discussed. Today's noisy devices cannot yet model real pharmaceutical molecules; serious value is widely placed in the 2030s. We study where it genuinely helps — and where it does not yet.

diffraction comb

Field D · Quantum

Biomedical Engineering × Quantum Convergence

The convergence is institutionalizing at top universities — quantum sensing for biomagnetic imaging, instrumentation, materials. Aspirational, early, and exactly the frontier this delegation is sent to read.

03 One Day, in Cross-section · 一日剖面

How a single day is built.

Morning is input; afternoon is output — the program's pedagogy, made literal.

  1. Fig. 1 · AM

    Morning — input

    A faculty or speaker lecture, a laboratory visit, or a topic introduction. Take notes, record questions, prepare for the afternoon's output.

  2. Fig. 2 · PM

    Afternoon — output

    A daily teach-back: three groups present for eight to ten minutes each, then five minutes of Q&A — explaining the day's learning in their own words. Then project studio.

  3. Fig. 3 · Close

    Daily closing

    A traceable learning log for each delegate: three takeaways, one still-confusing question, and one next step.

  4. Fig. 4 · Weekend

    Weekends

    Self-guided city and innovation exploration, rest, and personal reflection. No formal output — not make-up class time.
